<div>
  <h2>📋 Task Description</h2>
  <p>
    <strong>Objective:</strong> Create a prompt that transforms a basic job description into a tailored resume summary. Include the role, target company type, years of experience, and desired format (bullet points with metrics).
  </p>
  <p>
    This assignment focuses on designing a context-rich prompt that forces the AI to output a highly specific and professional resume snippet, validating the effectiveness of structured constraints and persona assignment.
  </p>
</div>

<br/>

<div>
  <h2>🎯 What We Learnt</h2>
  <p>
    Through completing this section of the assignment, several key techniques from our coursework were actively applied and verified.
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Techniques Applied:</strong> Role-Based Prompting and Few-Shot Learning.</li>
    <h3><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h3>
    <li>Clear role definition (e.g., “resume expert”) significantly improves output quality.</li>
    <li>Adding constraints like bullet points and word limits makes responses more structured.</li>
    <li>Including metrics (numbers, impact) makes resumes stronger and more professional.</li>
    <li>Providing job-specific context helps generate targeted and relevant summaries.</li>
    <li>Well-defined prompts lead to concise, ATS-friendly content</li> 
    <h3><strong>Observations:</strong></h3>
    <li>Basic prompts result in generic and less effective resume outputs</li>
<li>Refined prompts produce more personalized and achievement-focused content</li>
<li>Adding keywords improves matching with job descriptions</li>
<li>Structured prompts reduce vague and unnecessary information</li>
<li>Prompt clarity directly enhances output quality and relevance</li>
  </ul>
</div>

  <h2>📝 Analysis &amp; Reflection</h2>
  <p>Reflecting on the prompt engineering lifecycle to identify exactly what modifications led to performance gains.</p>
  
  <h3>What I Changed and Why</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>
      <strong>Modification 1:</strong> Removed complex or buzz-heavy wording
      <p><em>Reasoning:</em> Simple language feels more human and avoids AI detection patterns. Recruiters prefer clarity over fancy wording.</p>
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>Modification 2:</strong>Kept it under 40 words
      <p><em>Reasoning:</em> ATS systems and recruiters scan quickly. Short summaries improve readability and retention.</p>
    </li>
  </ul>
