<div>
  <h2>📋 Task Description</h2>
  <p>
    <strong>Objective:</strong> Create prompts for 3 different email scenarios: cold outreach, follow-up after a meeting, and requesting a deadline extension. Each should specify tone, length, and call-to-action.
  </p>
  <p>
    This assignment focuses on designing context-rich prompts that force the AI to output highly specific and professional emails, validating the effectiveness of structured constraints and distinct formatting rules.
  </p>
</div>

<br/>

<div>
  <h2>🎯 What We Learnt</h2>
  <p>
    Through completing this section of the assignment, several key techniques from our coursework were actively applied and verified.
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Techniques Applied:</strong> Role-Based Prompting and Constraints.</li>
    <li><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong> The quality of a cold referral email depends on how specific and concise it is.</li>
    <li>Less information → generic template.</li>
    <li>More details + clear constraints → a strong, natural, and effective email that actually gets read and replied to.</li>
  </ul>
</div>

  <h2>📝 Analysis &amp; Reflection</h2>
  <p>Reflecting on the prompt engineering lifecycle to identify exactly what modifications led to performance gains.</p>
  
  <h3>What I Changed and Why</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>
      <strong>Modification 1:</strong> First prompt was only “Write a cold email for referal in amazon.” Second prompt added full candidate details, exact skills (React, Tailwind), role (MERN stack), links, projects, and word limit.
      <p><em>Reasoning:</em> To make the email specific to the candidate’s real background instead of a generic template.</p>
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>Modification 2:</strong>Second prompt added strict style rules (under 120 words, concise, to the point, no buzzy words, end with CTA) plus a new request to analyze the two prompts.
      <p><em>Reasoning:</em> To get a shorter, cleaner email that has higher reply chances from Amazon employees.</p>
    </li>
  </ul>
